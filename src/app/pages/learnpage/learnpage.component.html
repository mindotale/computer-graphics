Fractals are geometric patterns characterized by self-similarity, meaning they exhibit similar structures at various scales. These intricate and often beautiful shapes are generated through mathematical formulas and iterative processes. Notable examples include the Mandelbrot set, Julia set, Sierpinski triangle, and the Koch snowflake.

Fractals

A Newton fractal is a type of fractal generated through iterative root-finding using Newton's method, a numerical technique for finding roots of a real-valued function

Viecsek fractal

The Vicsek fractal, often associated with the Vicsek snowflake, is created by recursively replacing equilateral triangles with smaller equilateral triangles.

Chaos theory

Chaos theory is a branch of mathematics and physics that studies the behavior of dynamic systems that are highly sensitive to initial conditions.
